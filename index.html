Why SBfinance

Most teams have data — but not a model they trust. Forecasting becomes “best guesses”, risks go unnoticed, and decisions happen late.

The problem

  • Data is scattered across spreadsheets, exports, and tools.
  • Forecasts are fragile and break when reality changes.
  • Risk is invisible until you hit the wall (cash, churn, margin).

The solution

  • Ingest raw data and normalize it automatically.
  • Build a forecasting model (and keep it updated).
  • Explain results in chat, with risks + recommended actions.

Who it’s for

  • SMBs, startups, finance leads, ops teams.
  • Anyone who needs answers fast — without a data team.
  • Teams who want BI-level visibility + narrative reasoning.

FAQ

Quick answers to the questions people ask before they trust finance tooling.

What data do you need to get started?
CSV/Excel exports are enough: revenue, expenses, cash, and (optionally) customer metrics. Start messy — we can refine the schema over time.
Is this just another dashboard?
Dashboards show “what happened.” SBfinance focuses on “what will happen” and “what to do next” — with chat explanations, scenario stress tests, and risk notes.
How do you handle uncertainty?
Forecasts are estimates. We surface assumptions, highlight risk drivers, and encourage scenario planning instead of pretending the future is deterministic.
Can I export results to Power BI / Tableau?
Yes — SBfinance can produce BI-ready outputs (e.g., CSV tables) to plug into your visualization tool of choice.
Is SBfinance financial advice?
No. It’s analytics + recommendations based on your inputs. You remain responsible for decisions.
